---
clade: Megaraptora
parent: coelurosauria
image: https://alexjercan.github.io/metajurassic-images/clades/megaraptora.svg
---

Diagnosed by highly pneumatized, lightweight skeletons and significantly elongated, muscular arms ending in disproportionately large claws on the first and second fingers. Instead of relying heavily on their jaws, they utilized these massive hands to grapple, slash, and bleed out medium-sized prey, effectively replacing the carcharodontosaurids as apex predators in Late Cretaceous South America and Australia.
