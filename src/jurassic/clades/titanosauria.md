Diagnosed by an exceptionally broad pelvis creating a "wide-gauge" skeletal posture, and the complete loss of ossified wrist bones, forcing them to walk entirely on the vertical columns of their metacarpals. This widened stance provided the essential structural stability needed to support massive weight, allowing them to expand globally and become the heaviest land animals in Earth's history.
