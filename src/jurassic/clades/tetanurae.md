Diagnosed by the reduction of the hand to three functional digits and a highly specialized tail structure with overlapping bony processes. This stiffened tail acted as a dynamic counterbalance—functioning much like a tightrope walker's pole—providing essential stability during high-speed, agile turns. Because this massive evolutionary split encompasses all remaining theropods, it serves as the direct ancestral lineage for every bird alive today.
