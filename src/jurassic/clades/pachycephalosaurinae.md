Diagnosed by the complete obliteration of the upper temporal fenestrae (skull openings) as the solid bone dome fully expands and seals in adults, alongside a reduction of peripheral skull spikes. Because their skulls changed so drastically as they grew—absorbing their juvenile spikes into a massive, smooth adult dome—paleontologists initially mistook their different growth stages for completely different species, famously misidentifying them as Dracorex and Stygimoloch.
