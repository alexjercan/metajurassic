clade: Neornithischia
parent: genasauria

Diagnosed by an asymmetrical distribution of enamel on their cheek teeth—thicker on the inside of the lower teeth and the outside of the upper teeth. As they chewed, this uneven thickness caused the teeth to wear down at different rates, creating perpetually self-sharpening ridges that allowed them to effortlessly slice through tough, fibrous vegetation.
