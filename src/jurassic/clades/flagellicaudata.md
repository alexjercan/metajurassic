clade: Flagellicaudata
parent: diplodocoidea
image: https://alexjercan.github.io/metajurassic-images/clades/flagellicaudata.svg
---

Diagnosed by a tail tapering into a whip composed of up to 80 simple, cylindrical vertebrae. Because muscle attachments stopped well before the end of the tail, this terminal section functioned as a highly flexible, lightweight whip, giving them an advantage as an extended sensory tool or a fast-cracking defensive weapon.
