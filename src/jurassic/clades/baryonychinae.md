Diagnosed by a slightly curved, rosette-shaped tip of the snout and the retention of more robust, finely serrated teeth compared to their highly specialized relatives. This intermediate anatomical stage kept them opportunistic, using their enormous thumb claws to hook fish out of the water while still remaining highly capable of scavenging or hunting land-dwelling prey.
