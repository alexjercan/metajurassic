clade: Thyreophora
parent: genasauria

---

Diagnosed by the presence of parallel longitudinal rows of bony scutes (osteoderms) running down the neck, back, and tail. This heavy dermal armor provided a formidable passive defense against the slashing teeth of early theropod predators, effectively turning them into walking fortresses that could comfortably browse low-lying vegetation in highly dangerous ecosystems.
