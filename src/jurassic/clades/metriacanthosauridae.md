---
clade: Metriacanthosauridae
parent: allosauroidea
---

Diagnosed by a heavily built, robust skull and prominent bony ridges running along the top of the snout. These reinforced cranial structures allowed them to tackle large, armored prey, making them the undisputed apex predators of the Middle Jurassic to Early Cretaceous across Europe and Asia before being outcompeted by more advanced lineages.
