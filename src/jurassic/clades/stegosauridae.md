Diagnosed by the complete loss of front teeth (replaced entirely by a keratinous beak) and hindlimbs that are drastically longer than their forelimbs. This extreme difference in leg length forced them into a permanent, steep downward-sloping posture, biologically locking their feeding zone exclusively to low-growing ferns and cycads—essentially turning them into the Mesozoic equivalent of heavy grazing rhinos.
