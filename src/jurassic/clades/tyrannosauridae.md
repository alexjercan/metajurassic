Diagnosed by a dramatically widened back of the skull (creating forward-facing eyes) and the strict reduction of the hand to only two functional fingers. The widened skull provided exceptional binocular vision for precise depth perception, while simultaneously anchoring immense jaw muscles capable of biting straight through solid bone.
