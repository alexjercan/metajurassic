clade: Clypeodontia
parent: neornithischia
image: https://alexjercan.github.io/metajurassic-images/clades/clypeodontia.svg
---

Diagnosed by a heavily pronounced, primary vertical ridge running down the outside (labial side) of their upper teeth. This structural reinforcement acted like a heavy buttress, preventing the teeth from shattering under immense biting pressure as these animals evolved increasingly complex, powerful jaws to process massive quantities of plant matter.
