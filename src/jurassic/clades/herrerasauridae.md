Diagnosed by a confusing mosaic of primitive and advanced traits. They retained an ancestral pelvis with only two sacral vertebrae and a hand where the third finger is longest, yet possessed an advanced sliding lower jaw. Because of this, their placement is highly controversial. For this game, we followed the Langer consensus, classifying them as basal saurischians. However, the debate rages on: other researchers argue they are basal theropods, and some modern studies suggest they might sit entirely outside of Dinosauria!
