Diagnosed by a heavily fused, single dome-like horn on the frontal bones of the skull. Because they were geographically restricted to the island of Madagascar, this strict confinement made them the undisputed apex predators of an isolated ecosystem—a dominance so absolute that matching tooth marks on their own bones provide definitive fossil evidence of cannibalism.
