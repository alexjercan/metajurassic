Diagnosed by a highly stiffened tail bound by interlocking bony rods, alongside a significantly enlarged sickle claw on the second toe. The stiff tail acted as a rigid counterbalance during high-speed turns, granting them extreme precision while actively pursuing and leaping onto prey.
