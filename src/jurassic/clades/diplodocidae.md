Diagnosed by the extreme elongation of both cervical and caudal vertebrae. Their nasal openings migrated completely to the apex of the skull, sitting directly above the eyes. This dorsal placement kept their airway completely clear, allowing them to breathe freely without inhaling dust, dirt, or water while their snouts were continuously buried in low-lying vegetation.
