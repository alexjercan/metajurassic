Diagnosed by a transition to 'graviportal' or pillar-like limbs, shifting their legs directly under their bodies to support enormous weight, similar to modern elephants. To lighten their massive frames, they evolved 'hollow' neck vertebrae filled with air sacs—an anatomical trick that allowed them to reach lengths and weights that no other land animal has ever matched.
