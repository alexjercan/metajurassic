clade: Albertosaurinae
parent: tyrannosauridae
---

Diagnosed by a notably more slender, gracile build compared to the heavier tyrannosaurines, and proportionally longer lower leg bones (the tibia and metatarsals). This highly specialized leg anatomy made them the fastest runners among the large Late Cretaceous apex predators, allowing them to effectively chase down swift-moving hadrosaurs over open ground rather than relying strictly on ambush tactics.
