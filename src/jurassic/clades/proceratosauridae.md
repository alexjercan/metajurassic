---
clade: Proceratosauridae
parent: tyrannosauroidea
---

Diagnosed by elaborate, delicate bony crests on the snout and a generally small, lightly built body plan. Because these early tyrannosauroids were outsized by massive allosauroids and megalosaurs, their lightweight build allowed them to exploit a secondary predatory niche, hunting smaller, faster prey while using their elaborate crests for visual display rather than combat.
