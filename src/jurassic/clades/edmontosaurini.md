clade: Edmontosaurini
parent: saurolophinae
---

Diagnosed by an exceptionally long, flat, wide snout with a massive, shovel-like beak and the complete absence of any bony cranial crest. To compensate for the lack of a bony crest, perfectly preserved "mummy" fossils reveal they possessed a fleshy, soft-tissue comb on top of their heads—like a rooster—used for bright, visual herd signaling while dominating the northern floodplains.
