---
clade: Nodosauridae
parent: ankylosauria
image: https://alexjercan.github.io/metajurassic-images/clades/nodosauridae.svg
---

Diagnosed by the distinct lack of a tail club, the presence of enormous parasagittal spikes (especially massive over the shoulders), and a noticeably narrow, elongated snout. Their narrow snouts allowed for highly selective feeding, acting like biological tweezers to pick out specific, nutrient-rich plants, while their massive shoulder spikes protected their vulnerable necks from the slashing bites of predators.
