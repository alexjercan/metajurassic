clade: Allosauroidea
parent: avetheropoda
---

Diagnosed by relatively narrow, lightly built skulls paired with highly flexible neck joints and jaws capable of opening to extreme angles. Instead of relying on crushing bite force, they employed a "hatchet-bite" feeding strategy, using their upper jaws to drive serrated teeth into prey and pull back rapidly to inflict massive, incapacitating flesh wounds.
