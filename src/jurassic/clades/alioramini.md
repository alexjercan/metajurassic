clade: Alioramini
parent: tyrannosaurinae
---

Diagnosed by extremely long, narrow snouts lined with a higher number of smaller, sharper teeth, and a series of distinct bony bumps along the top of the snout. Because they lived directly alongside massive, bone-crushing tyrannosaurines in Asia, their delicate, elongated skulls allowed them to avoid direct competition by hunting smaller, faster prey that the heavyweights couldn't catch.
