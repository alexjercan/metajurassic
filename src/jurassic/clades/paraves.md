Diagnosed by a retractable, sickle-shaped claw on the second toe of the foot and backward-directed pubic bones. The hyper-extendable toe claw functioned as a specialized weapon for puncturing or pinning down struggling prey, while the shifted pelvis altered their center of mass to drastically improve balance and agility.
