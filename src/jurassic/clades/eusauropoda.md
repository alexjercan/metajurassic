clade: Eusauropoda
parent: sauropoda

Diagnosed by a radical restructuring of the hands and skull. Their front digits fused into a rigid, vertical semi-circle (resembling a horseshoe) that functioned as a solid weight-bearing pillar, losing almost all individual flexibility. Simultaneously, their nasal openings migrated backward from the tip of the snout toward the top of the skull, a definitive skeletal marker for all advanced long-necks.
