Diagnosed by forelimbs significantly longer than their hindlimbs, creating a permanently upward-sloping back. This elevated posture granted uncontested access to the highest forest canopies, avoiding competition with lower-browsing herbivores, though it required extreme cardiovascular pressure to pump blood vertically to the brain.
