Diagnosed by an elongated, low-slung skull and a significantly lengthened neck. This family is famous for dominating the Late Triassic fossil beds of Europe and South America, where hundreds of individuals have been found together in "bone beds." These mass-accumulation sites suggest they traveled in large herds, using their specialized, mobile thumb claws to hook and pull down high-growing vegetation that smaller herbivores simply couldn't reach.
